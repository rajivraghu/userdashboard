Mission: Impossible is an American action spy film franchise based on the 1960s television series of the same name created by Bruce Geller.
The film series, starring Tom Cruise as IMF agent Ethan Hunt, is known for its high-stakes espionage plots, daring stunts, and elaborate set pieces.
Since the first installment released in 1996, the franchise has expanded to include multiple sequels, each showcasing increasingly ambitious action sequences and globe-trotting storylines.
Celebrated for Cruise's commitment to performing his own stunts, Mission: Impossible has become one of the highest-grossing and most enduring action franchises in cinema history.
